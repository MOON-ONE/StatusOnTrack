Evaluation


Introduction

After fixing our key tasks and finishing the storyboard and prototypes, we need to conduct a formative evaluation before we finalize our prototype and dive into development process. We choose think aloud as our evaluation method, each user will speak out their thoughts/comments upon the mock system they are interacting with. From the user’s experience, we can refine the statements he or she makes and list all the advantages and disadvantages of each prototype, which leads to our retrospective review and final prototype building. Besides, the user may also give us valuable suggestion as he or she is playing around the system. In order to capture all the useful points from the user, we have two members in charge of notes-taking, we also take audio recording of the user in case we miss some of the important points in our side notes. Finally, we will analyze the points we take down, summarize it, and build a pros and cons table.

Major findings

Search

1. Search bar should be placed on top of the page. The search button will 
   contain a dropdown list, in which user can specify various search criteria.
   
2. User can select multiple search results, and toggle the visibility of 
   unselected ones.
Compare

1. The column name should be clickable. Once clicked, the system will sort 
   the items according to the value of this column. Keep clicking will reverse 
   the order.
   
2. Smart sorting: ranks are calculated from price and condition.

3. Statistics: average, minimum, maximum price and total number of books (All
   figures are calculated based on current visible result).
   
4. When a seller is filling the book info, relative statistics will be shown. 
   (A background search is performed by the system, and the values shown is 
   calculated based on the search result)
Post
            
1. In some of our prototypes, the posts have been categorized into “selling 
   wishes” and “buying wishes”. However, base on the evaluation, most of the 
   users will ignore the “buying wishes” section. They tend to use the search 
   functionality to find what they want. Therefore, we decide to remain only 
   selling wishes section. However, in some special cases, the user may not 
   find what they want. In this case, we allow the user to add it to the his/
   her wish list. Later if some one post a qualified book on the forum, the 
   user will be notified (by email, etc.)

2. We find that we may include the following information:
    2.1 Book Specifics
        2.1.1 Module Code (*)
        2.1.2 Book Title (*)
        2.1.3 Author
        2.1.4 Edition
        2.1.5 Condition: Checklist & a figure from 1 to 10
        2.1.6 Photo: upload from desktop or smart phone
    2.2 Contact Specifics
        2.2.1 Phone (*)
        2.2.2 Email
    2.3 Order Specifics
        2.3.1 Price
        2.3.2 Remark
        
3. A preview will be shown for confirmation before the post is actually 
   recorded in the database.
Account

(This is not mentioned in the three key tasks above, but we find that it is closely related to them during the user evaluation. Therefore we put it here as a major finding or a requirement for our G3 high-fidelity prototype.) 


1. User can modified their contacts. (Which will be used to pre-fill 
   corresponding fields when they create a post)

2. User can view their historical posts, renew them or mark them with 
   “Sold out” or “Close”.

Summary & Reflection


In G2, each of the team member has come up with an low-fidelity prototype. We evaluated each of the prototypes with 4 of our target users. In the process of evaluation, the author of the prototype helped the user to carry on with simulation while the rest of the team record the words and behaviors of the user. It’s a formative evaluation, we will improve our designs with the feedback of current prototypes from the potential users.

During the evaluation process, user will “think aloud” - to tell us how he/she is thinking or feeling and help us to understand more about the experience the system bring to him/her. The overall evaluation result is acceptable: we conclude the pros and cons from different prototype, list them in a form for easy comparison and we finally combine the good features from the 4 individual prototype to construct a single and integrate one.

We are glad that we have always kept the two personas we came up with in Step One in our minds. The whole evaluation process is carried out with respects to the three main task from the two persona, namely searching, posting and comparing. We asked every testing user to proceed the three tasks and record their opinions. Nevertheless, we do not restrict them from side-lines: users can always give us whatever feedbacks or comments they want to give.

However, there are problems occurring during the evaluation process. The most severe one is that the quality of prototypes is not good enough. Only very basic low-fi paper prototypes are built to cover the main three task. Although this is the requirements specified inside the project manual and it might be good enough for us to burst some creative ideas, personally I think such low fidelity will bring in very much distraction when we are actually carrying out the evaluation. Most of the users cannot directly understand the meaning for the various components in the prototype. They tend to ask the designer to explain the interface. Without the explanation from the designer, it is very hard for users to find out how the system works. It is because low fidelity and incompleteness of the prototype do not allow them to do the trail & error explorations. However, if the designer explain how the system works, then it seems the purpose of evaluation is violated because during the actual use, designers cannot be aside to every user to guide them through the system. As this stage, personally we think doing an expert evaluation is more appropriate since we can explain the work flow of the system to experts and then they can evaluate the system with the heuristics and their own expertise. Such evaluation is much more insusceptible to the fidelity of prototype than those walkthrough evaluations involving actual users.
