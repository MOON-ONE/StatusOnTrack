Background

Jason is a year four computer science undergraduate in NUS. Since his study focus lies into a computer-related area, he interacts with computers every and is very familiar with the computer systems. He has been in NUS for seven semesters and has taken over thirty modules. Among those modules, over 70% requires one or more textbooks for the students to follow. Jason thus has accumulated a large bunch of textbooks of the modules he has taken. Hence, he decides to sell those used-textbooks instead throwing into into trash bins directly. He tried a few online used textbook trading platforms, but very few juniors contact him and ask about the textbooks.


Goals
  • Primary Goal
    • Selling certain used textbooks
  • Sub-Goals
    • Post Textbook Information:
      “I would like to post textbook information and my contact information on the platform in order to advertising my book. These info may include the book title, the module it is used in, a picture of the cover, its author, version, condition and my phone numbers.”

    • View the popularity of a certain textbook:
      “It would be great if I can see the need of the books I want to sell in the market and check if it has already been in someone’s wishing list. It’s even better if I can know the wisher’s name and contact info, since I would contact them directly rather than wait for some unknown buyers to contact me.”

    • View the average price of a certain textbook:
      “I don’t really know how to price my book. If I can see the average price of the existing ones, maybe it would be easier for me to set my price.”

    • Share the post:
      “Sometimes I want to post the selling info to my social networks, but it’s really boring and troublesome to enter all those information again. It would be much more convenient if there is a button for me to share the post directly.”

Background

Lucy has just joined NUS and she doesn’t know many seniors in the university. She is majored in Chinese Study in Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences and thus she is not very familiar with the computer systems. In the first lectures of the semester, she was told that she would need to buy the textbooks used in the module to catch up with the lecturer’s teaching progress. However, when she walked into bookstores and finally find the right book, she was shocked by the high price. As a result, she turns to seek a platform to buy the used-textbooks in good condition with much lower price. She has tried the textbook forum provided by the school. However, when she logged into the page, she was confused and didn’t know where to start. After several tries, she found the textbook which she wanted. However, after she left a message under the post, she never got replies. She guessed that the seller might have already sold the book or he/she never check the post again.


Goals
  • Primary Goal
    • Buy certain used-textbooks
  • Sub-Goals
    • Search for the book:
      “The most important thing for me is to find my desired book. Sometimes I may need to search for the module code of the corresponding module, or I may need to directly search the book’s name.”
    • Compare the price:
      “After all, the reason why I chose to buy a used-textbook is its low price. It would be great if the qualified books (after searching) can be sorted with respect to their price. Then It would be easier for me to choose only from the cheapest ones.”
    • Compare the condition:
      “Some books with super low price are in very bad conditions and this is not the kind I want. I want to be able to compare the condition of the books and choose the one with best condition and reasonable price.”
    • Contact with seller effectively:
      “I could not get the response from the seller in time. I would like to know whether the item has been sold and I would like to contact the seller in a more effecient way. ”



Storyboard

Key tasks
Searching for particular items

In order to display the information the users want to see, a powerful searching function is indispensable. User should be able to express their criteria and filter the results so that they can retrieve the desired content from the textbook forum.

Comparing among different items

After the user finished searching, the results should be clearly listed and the key attributes of the tasks should be placed appropriately so that the user can compare one of the item with each others, thus make decision on which one should he/she buy.

Posting buying/selling information

If the user failed to find the existing post that match his requirement, he or she can create a new post and leave the specific information of the textbook he or she wants to buy/sell. The user might need to get the notification in time if there is something happening related to his/her post so that the user could do further operation without long delay.

Major findings

Search

1. Search bar should be placed on top of the page. The search button will 
   contain a dropdown list, in which user can specify various search criteria.
   
2. User can select multiple search results, and toggle the visibility of 
   unselected ones.
Compare

1. The column name should be clickable. Once clicked, the system will sort 
   the items according to the value of this column. Keep clicking will reverse 
   the order.
   
2. Smart sorting: ranks are calculated from price and condition.

3. Statistics: average, minimum, maximum price and total number of books (All
   figures are calculated based on current visible result).
   
4. When a seller is filling the book info, relative statistics will be shown. 
   (A background search is performed by the system, and the values shown is 
   calculated based on the search result)
Post
            
1. In some of our prototypes, the posts have been categorized into “selling 
   wishes” and “buying wishes”. However, base on the evaluation, most of the 
   users will ignore the “buying wishes” section. They tend to use the search 
   functionality to find what they want. Therefore, we decide to remain only 
   selling wishes section. However, in some special cases, the user may not 
   find what they want. In this case, we allow the user to add it to the his/
   her wish list. Later if some one post a qualified book on the forum, the 
   user will be notified (by email, etc.)

2. We find that we may include the following information:

Summary & Reflection


In G2, each of the team member has come up with an low-fidelity prototype. We evaluated each of the prototypes with 4 of our target users. In the process of evaluation, the author of the prototype helped the user to carry on with simulation while the rest of the team record the words and behaviors of the user. It’s a formative evaluation, we will improve our designs with the feedback of current prototypes from the potential users.

During the evaluation process, user will “think aloud” - to tell us how he/she is thinking or feeling and help us to understand more about the experience the system bring to him/her. The overall evaluation result is acceptable: we conclude the pros and cons from different prototype, list them in a form for easy comparison and we finally combine the good features from the 4 individual prototype to construct a single and integrate one.

We are glad that we have always kept the two personas we came up with in Step One in our minds. The whole evaluation process is carried out with respects to the three main task from the two persona, namely searching, posting and comparing. We asked every testing user to proceed the three tasks and record their opinions. Nevertheless, we do not restrict them from side-lines: users can always give us whatever feedbacks or comments they want to give.

However, there are problems occurring during the evaluation process. The most severe one is that the quality of prototypes is not good enough. Only very basic low-fi paper prototypes are built to cover the main three task. Although this is the requirements specified inside the project manual and it might be good enough for us to burst some creative ideas, personally I think such low fidelity will bring in very much distraction when we are actually carrying out the evaluation. Most of the users cannot directly understand the meaning for the various components in the prototype. They tend to ask the designer to explain the interface. Without the explanation from the designer, it is very hard for users to find out how the system works. It is because low fidelity and incompleteness of the prototype do not allow them to do the trail & error explorations. However, if the designer explain how the system works, then it seems the purpose of evaluation is violated because during the actual use, designers cannot be aside to every user to guide them through the system. As this stage, personally we think doing an expert evaluation is more appropriate since we can explain the work flow of the system to experts and then they can evaluate the system with the heuristics and their own expertise. Such evaluation is much more insusceptible to the fidelity of prototype than those walkthrough evaluations involving actual users.
