Design Problem Overview

At the beginning of each semester, almost every NUS student will encounter a problem: purchasing textbooks. However, based on our research, more than 72% of textbooks in Co-op cost more than 40 SGD. Some are even more than 60 SGD. The high prices lead to the birth of IVLE used-textbook market.

According to our research, there are generally three ways for NUS students to buy or sell their used textbooks.

1. 10%: from friends/senior/juniors

2. 10%: via Carousell

3. 80%: via IVLE used-textbook forum

It is obvious that IVLE used textbook forum are the main platform for used textbooks exchanging.

However, based on our personal experiences and some complaint from classmates, our team find there are a lot of design problems with the forum. These problems include the confusing user interface design, defective functionalities, etc. These problems waste users’ time and bring in awful user experiences. Hence, our team would like to put our effort in the production of a new used textbook exchange platform.


Target Users

NUS students who need to buy or sell their used textbooks

The aim of our project is to understand the existing design problems of the current IVLE used textbook forum and finally redesign it or produce a new platform. So we choose those students who need to buy or sell their used textbooks, especially for students who prefer to use an online platform to trade as our target users. Therefore, our target users are NUS students, either undergraduate or graduate students, who need to buy or sell secondhand textbooks.

We have a large group of target users for two reasons. On one hand, since some of the textbooks can be quite expensive, some students, may prefer to buy a used textbooks from other students. On the other hand, some textbooks are only used by one specific module, students may not use them again once they have finished that module. In this case, some students may choose to sell the textbooks to other students. Therefore, these two groups of students may both become our potential users.

reflection

The very first contextual inquiry is not as easy as we expected. We assigned two pals to be the interviewers and the rest two to be the note takers. This strategy has its own pros and cons. For the positive side, two interviewers created much more “run-time” ideas than only one: it is because for the same user behavior, two interviewers may have two completely different sets of questions, resulting in a wider inquiry coverage. However, some issues may occur on the coordination of the two interviewers. Sometimes, one interviewer may be interrupted by the other and thus the pace of the whole interview may go out of control. Maybe next time we’ll take a modified way: assign one teammate as the main interviewer, and assign another as “co-interviewer”. The co-interviewer will take notes of the questions he/she comes up during the interview and ask the interviewee when the main interviewer finishes his/her question sets. Or the co-interviewer will ask the interviewee in the end of the interview as complementary questions.

Most of the problems of the existing systems are revealed during the contextual inquiry and there are some wonderful ideas which we had never thought of before. Interestingly, such shining ideas are always from some subliminal words of the users instead of those formal answers. The viewpoints of our interviewees are greatly different with respect to their majors. The findings from the more professional interviewees (computer science students in this case) are usually the technical ones, such as the bugs or the poor performance of the system while other responses tend to be more general or intuitive.

The time periods the interview carried out introduces a lot of difficulties. Since it’s in the right middle of a semester, there are very few users using the used-text book forum. In other words, we had to ask the interviewees to pretend they are buying or selling their used textbooks. Although we created some posts on the existing forum in order to simulate a relative real situation, yet there is still a gulf between the contextual inquiry and actual cases. Another difficulty we met is the time length of the interview. Since everybody in NUS has a fast pace, it is not easy to ask a stranger to stall and accept a thirty-minute interview, even with rewards (We did prepare some rewards).

We did ask both general and specific questions. Some questions like “Does the interface bring troubles to you” will definitely gives very abstract answers. Although some specific questions may have better effects, yet we found that a demo is much more useful than a hundred questions. The demo will force the users to be concentrated. Furthermore, the demo process may reveal problems which interviewees usually ignore in every day life. Such problems are the ones that users usually are not aware of. However, they will appreciate it a lot if these problems are well solved. In other words, users are among the ones we value the most.

Of course, there are easy moments during the proceeding of such contextual inquiries. We enjoyed the process talking with end users. We were also surprised and excited at the ideas and new design options brought by them. The first step is taken and we are looking forward to the next.

Data Analysis

briefing

Briefly, we went through the following post-interview processes:

1. refine field notes/sketches, audio recording from the interviews
2. write all outcomes of (1) on memopads
3. arrange and  categorize the memopads to generate the affinity diagram
4. extract key observations from the affinity diagram
5. start from key observation, iterate through the hierarchy of key 
   observations, user statements, breakdown, insights and until we
   get the design ideas.

* During the above process, if we encountered any problems or ambiguities,
we would return to the raw data to confirm the interviewee's meaning


detailed process

As soon as the interviews were over, the audio records and field notes were gathered together and settled according to the four interviewees we got. The thing we went through is to refine these field notes/sketches: write all the points down on memopads, labelled them in a consistant format and then finally organized them in 14 different categories (in total 101 items). 

01. Accessibility(7)
02. User Interface (20)
03. Responsiveness (7)
04. User Actions (7)
05. Alternatives (6)
06. Multiple Platforms Support (4)
07. Privacy (1)
08. Sorting (6)
09. User Expections (12)
10. Redundant & Confusing Functionalities (7)
11. Searching (4)
12. Communication (4)
13. Attachment (6)
14. Information Format (10)
An affinity diagram was generated by the end of this organizing process.

1. One key observation is the first problem our interviewee raised is always about UI. There are several UI problems which are mentioned by the interviewees with high frequency. For example, 3 of our interviewees said that there are too many useless buttons inside the post/reply box, especially for audio attachment function. They have no idea why we need an audio attachment for selling or buying used textbooks. With so many buttons, the whole post/reply box looks very messy. Almost all of our interviewees prefer a neat interface.

Another issue is that the whole design for the forum is not attractive. Everything inside the forum has an IVLE design style which is not that colorful and impressive. Especially, user2 mentioned that he may not want to use the old style forum if there is a newer and nicer one.

Last but not the least, UI design in the forum do not point out those information users are really interested in. For example, user4 would like to see the price and title information directly in the searching result lists. However, what she can see is only the module code and seller’s name. She have to click on the link and check the information inside. User2 and user4 also mentioned that there are only 10 posts per page, which is somewhat not enough. They want more results to be in one page.

Some typical user statements about UI problems include: “The interface should be as neat as possible. The number of buttons should also be minimized.” “I don’t know the functionality of most buttons” “The UI is not attractive and not colorful. ” “Some information that provided by users are not that obvious ”

Hence, to make a breakdown. UI problems can be generally divided into three categories. Firstly, there are some redundant functions and icons which makes the interface become very messy. Secondly, the interface does not provide sufficient information. For example, price is an important figure which is valued by users a lot, but it is not shown in the front page. Thirdly, the whole interface is not attractive and colorful.

So from these three points, we can get some insights. What users want is a neat, nice platform. The do not want a lot of gaudy but useless functions. Using a software is an intuitive process, so users will mostly focus on what they see and what they can use. If there are many different kinds of functions or buttons, they may be lost since they are distracted by those unnecessary functions.

Therefore, when designing a new used textbook platform, we should minimize our buttons and functions but at the same time provide significant information intuitively. The whole interface should look neat and nice, which requires us to do a lot of work on layout and graphic design.

However, there are still questions like what information users are really care about. From our research, now we know price and textbooks title are something users care a lot. But what about other information? Are there any other information that users are greatly interested in? This may need further exploration since we do not do a lot of research on this area.

What tasks are desired?

1. Users can conveniently access to the tools;
2. Users use the powerful search function and indicate the information of the textbook they are going to search;
3. Users compare the searched results;
4. Users enter the chosen post, contact the seller;
5. The rest should be similar to the cases mentioned in current situation.


How are the tasks learned?

1. The information that users need to know is how to access to the certain important functionalities through the interface, and what output they are expected to get.
2. The users may learn the tasks through the user guide when they firstly open the application or webpage, or they just learnt from their friends’ experience.
3. The requirement for the users should be relatively low. Any user without systematic training should be guided by the user interface. However, they should have some basic understanding about the convention, eg. how a button looks like, what does a magnifier represent.


Where are the tasks performed?.

Places that users can access to the used textbook forum or Carousell, and meeting venues inside NUS or other meeting places outside, e.g MRT stations.


What's the relationship between user and data?

The users may access to the system via their own device or the commonly shared devices. Hence, for the private environment, the user data should be cached and the users do not need to log in every time. On the other hand, the private data in public environment should be cleared once the users exit from the system.
The system should retrieved the common data (available textbooks, textbook status) from the server dynamically. The data in the client side and the server site should be consistent.


What other tools does the user have?

Phones, used textbook forum and Carousell.


How do users communicate with each other?

Phone Calls, SMS, WhatsApp, Facebook, Wechat,…


How often are the tasks performed?

Normally a few times at the beginning of each semester


What are the time constraints on the task?

Users prefers wasting less time on waiting (for the page to be loaded and for the seller/buyer’s reply)


What happens when things go wrong?

If something unexpected happens and causes sellers and buyers cannot complete the trade, sellers will wait for another buyer and buyers will find another seller.


Requirement Analysis

The features are ordered bases on the number of concerns they raised.

A neat but elegant UI

Although we conduct interviews on only four target users, surprisingly there are twenty concerns (one fifth) are about the user interface. The word “nice”, “elegant” or “nice-looking” might be too general to conclude the exact design specifics. Therefore, we kept digging down this thread and found that users want the interface to contains the following detailed features:

clear

The important informations should be clear to users. For example, the prices or titles should be placed carefully so that the user do not have to switch between pages frequently to check these information

minimized

only show the core functionalities and necessary details, e.g. show only on-sale items, only commonly used buttons, etc .

intuitive
icons of the buttons should be reasonable enough to induce what will happen if user click it. Less intuitive icons should be grouped with text that is strong enough to tell the functionalities.
consistent

although it’s a new design, we still need to try to make it consistent to the existing systems. This will make it much easier for users to accept the new system.

self-explaining

since user will not bother to read a user guide before using the system, the system has to be self-explaining to some extent. For example, use placeholders to indicator the use of certain textfields, make reasonable titles for pages to help user navigate through the whole system.

Standards for information format

Among the 101 concerns, 10 are on the information format issues. These issues include user input standard and some unified standard about the book condition. Specifically,

create templates for user

When user want to sell a text book, they should be some post skeletons for users to choose. What they need to do then is just fill in the important figures, such as price, condition, contact, etc. This helps users to recognize information they need to input instead of recalling, thus it lowers the chance that a user miss some important information. These templates should separate the information user input to different sections, each of which has its unique id. This will reserve the space for data manipulations later.

A standard for book conditions

Sometimes descriptions like 9/10, 8/10 do not give the buyers enough information to know about the exact condition of the book. A standard, which could be measured in figures, is need to act as the ruler of such descriptions.

Responsiveness

7 concerns are on the responsiveness of system. Users are not satisfied with the connections speed of the existing forum. Something that we need to do include

Efficient algorithms to load views

This might be the main reason why current system operates so slow. We shall keep refine our algorithms to make the system scalable to large user groups.

Deploy on faster servers

This may be another factor that affects the performance of the website badly. We may want to deploy it on a faster server when it proves that the performance of current ones is the bottleneck of the system.

Picture Attachments

6 concerns are on attaching pictures to the post. User want a convenient way to upload and view images. Some requirements are:

Easy Uploading

A convenient way to upload pictures. Users need this functionality, but the super troublesome procedure of the currently system keeps them away from this. We need to brainstorm an idea to allow users to upload pictures easily with their smart phone.

Easy Viewing

Since we will support easy uploading, some posts may get many pictures attached inside. We need a organized way to display such pictures.

Search
Interestingly, only 4 concerns are about the searching functionality of the system, but all of our interviewees view searching as the most important functionality of a textbook forum. The requirements brought up by the users are:
Global Search

There should be a way to search among all the books available on the forum. Although we will have different categories, yet we have to provide a second way in case some sellers put their books in the wrong section.

Search for various attributes other than title and module

Some users have the need to search for a specific version of a book while others may need to search for authors. We need to extend the current search engine to accept keywords in more domains.

Specification of the relationship between keywords.

Some users may need only one of the keywords they input is satisfied by the shown results while others may require the results met all the keywords. We need to provide a way for the user to specify which relationship they want. (AND-relationship or OR-relationship)
